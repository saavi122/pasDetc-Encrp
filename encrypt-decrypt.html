Notes

Popular: AES is widely adopted for its security and performance. Some legacy algorithms (DES, RC4) are considered insecure. Key size influences security; larger keys generally increase resistance to brute-force attacks but may impact performance.

Quick history

Encryption has ancient roots (scytale, substitution ciphers), mechanical advances in the early 20th century (e.g., Enigma), and modern algorithmic systems (DES, AES) after the computer era.

Security tips

  • Use standard libraries (Web Crypto API, libsodium) — don’t roll your own crypto.
  • Prefer authenticated encryption (e.g., AES-GCM) to detect tampering.
  • Use sufficiently large keys (e.g., AES-256 when appropriate).
  • Rotate keys and protect secrets — never hardcode keys in client code.
